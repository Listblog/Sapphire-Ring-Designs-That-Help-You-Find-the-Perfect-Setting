Why Choose a Sapphire Ring?

Sapphires come in a stunning array of colours, excluding red, from classic blues to vibrant yellows, pinks, greens, and even rare shades like orange and violet. This spectrum of choice, combined with their exceptional durability, makes sapphires a symbol of both individuality and enduring value. Often chosen for engagement rings and heirloom jewellery, sapphires carry deep meaning and a rich history, making them a thoughtful choice for life’s most memorable moments.

  • Durability and Longevity:

    Sapphires rank 9 on the Mohs scale, making them one of the hardest gemstones after diamonds. Their toughness makes them ideal for daily wear, especially in rings.

  • A Rich History:

    Sapphires have been treasured since ancient times and were especially favoured in medieval Europe by clergy and royalty. One of the most iconic examples is Princess Diana’s sapphire engagement ring, now worn by Kate Middleton.

  • Symbolism and Meaning:

    Historically, sapphires were believed to offer protection and represent truth and wisdom. Today, they are often chosen for engagement rings as symbols of loyalty and enduring love.

How to Choose the Perfect Sapphire for Your Jewellery

Choosing the right sapphire for your jewellery is a personal decision that involves considering several factors. With expert advice and guidance, you can select a piece that suits your style and budget. Here are some essential aspects to keep in mind when choosing the perfect sapphire.

Sapphire Origin

Sapphires are found in several parts of the world, including Sri Lanka, Myanmar (Burma), and Madagascar. Australian sapphires are known for their unique colours, ranging from dark blue to vibrant greenish-blue. When selecting a sapphire, it’s essential to consider the origin of the stone, as different regions may produce sapphires with distinct characteristics.

Setting Style

The setting is another crucial factor when selecting your sapphire. There are various options available to complement the natural beauty of the stone, whether it’s a classic solitaire, a vintage-inspired halo, or a modern three-stone design. The setting should not only enhance the sapphire’s brilliance but also reflect the wearer’s personal style.

Budget

Sapphires are available at a range of price points, depending on their size, colour, and quality. When selecting a sapphire piece, it’s important to set a budget first. While certain sapphires can be more affordable than diamonds, larger and higher-quality stones will naturally come at a higher price. Our team can assist you in finding a piece that matches both your aesthetic desires and budget.
